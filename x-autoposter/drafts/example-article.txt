Your browser just became the most underrated AI tool you own.

Anthropic's Claude for Chrome extension is rolling out to paid subscribers, and it does something no chatbot has done for most people yet: it actually DOES things instead of just telling you how.

Here's what that means in practice.

Claude can now read the pages you have open, click buttons, fill out forms, and move between tabs — inside your own logged-in browser. Not a sandboxed demo. Your actual accounts, your actual tools.

Some real examples people are already using it for:

1. Research on autopilot. "Compare the pricing pages of these 5 tools and give me a table." Claude opens each one, reads them, and hands you the answer.

2. Form-filling drudgery. Expense reports, sign-ups, data entry between two systems that don't talk to each other. Claude does the clicking.

3. Recurring tasks on a schedule. Daily, weekly, monthly — set it once and Claude runs the same browser workflow automatically.

4. Inbox triage. Have it read a long email thread and draft the reply in your own tone, right in the compose window.

The catch? It's in beta, and you should treat it like a new intern: watch the first few runs before you let it work unsupervised. Anthropic added permission prompts before sensitive actions for exactly that reason.

But the direction is unmistakable. The gap between "AI that talks" and "AI that works" is closing fast — and it's closing inside the tab you already have open.

The move this week: pick ONE task you repeat in your browser every day and try handing it off. That's how you find out what an AI assistant is actually worth to you.
